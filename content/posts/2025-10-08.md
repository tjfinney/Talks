Here is a letter written to a teenager named Jay, who asked for a copy of the Bible.

## To Jay

> Dear Jay,

> I hope that this Bible will be a blessing to you. If you read it you will be blessed. It is a good book and a big book -- sixty or so books in fact. There are 39 books in the Old Testament and 27 in the New. That is a lot to get through. You don't have to start at the beginning and read all the way through. If it was me, I would start with one of the Gospels. Mark is a good place to start.

> All four Gospels are good (Matthew, Mark, Luke, John). It's good to read Genesis, Exodus, Deuteronomy, and all the rest but you don't have to read them all at once. Maybe just choose one verse (e.g. chapter three, verse 16 of each book that has three chapters and sixteen verses; chapter seven, verse 14 is good too) for each book of the whole Bible to get a taste.

> You will find yourself asking what something means. God is a spirit and God is the best teacher. Ask God to show you -- that's the best way.

> God bless you as you read the Bible.
